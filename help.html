Reading the top stat line

Example:

Time: 28673 Population: 450 Hunters: 10 Species: 91 Max generation: 17 Alerted: 41 Dominant: S1:256 S340:9 S900:8 S990:7

  • Time: Total simulation ticks elapsed since reset.
  • Population: Number of living organisms (prey/evolving species) currently on the map.
  • Hunters: Number of living red apex predators.
  • Species: Number of distinct species IDs alive right now.
  • Max generation: Highest generation value among living organisms.
  • Alerted: Organisms currently in fleeing state because a hunter is within their safety radius.
  • Dominant: Most numerous species at this moment, listed as S<speciesId>:<count>.

Breeding and population growth

Population grows through reproduction:

  • Organisms can now breed with nearby mature partners from the same species when both have enough energy and are not fleeing hunters.
  • Breeding blends parent traits, then applies mutation pressure from the current climate event. Offspring generation is parent max generation + 1.
  • If no mate is available, high-energy organisms can still reproduce alone.
  • Hunters also reproduce when they gain enough energy from successful hunts.
  • Population is capped by simulator limits, so growth eventually stabilizes under pressure.

Main visuals and indicators

  • Season pulse: A cyclic environment factor (shown as a number) that shifts nutrient growth up/down over time.
  • Background grid color: Nutrient density and climate tint. Brighter/greener cells usually provide more food.
  • Colored organisms: Evolving prey populations; color and shape indicate lineage and form progression.
  • Red triangular entities: Hunters (predators) that chase and drain prey energy.
  • Species tooltip (hover/click on organism): Species population, average generation, current state, and trait values.
  • Details line under stats: season pulse, current climate event, mutation pressure, average energy, births this frame, apex hunter generation, and dominant body forms.
